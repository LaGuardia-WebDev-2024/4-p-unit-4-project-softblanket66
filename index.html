Recipe #1

Xiao Long Bao, or Soup Dumplings, originated from the Jiangnan region of China, particularly Shanghai and Wuxi, and are renowned for their delicate, thin skin and flavorful broth-filled filling, traditionally steamed in small bamboo baskets.

Meal Ingredients
Xiao Long Bao (Soup Dumplings)
    Dough:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup boiling water
    • Filling:
  • 1 pound ground pork (or chicken)
  • 1/4 cup ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup scallions, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 cup chicken broth (gelatinized if possible)

Step 1: Prepare the Dough: Combine 2 cups of flour with 1/2 cup of boiling water, then gradually add 1/4 cup of cold water and knead until smooth, resting the dough for 30 minutes under a damp cloth.

Step 2:Make the Filling: Mix 1 pound of ground pork, 1/4 cup each of minced ginger and chopped scallions, 1/4 cup soy sauce, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, 1 tablespoon sugar, 1 teaspoon white pepper, and 1 cup of chicken broth until well combined.

Step 3:Shape the Dumplings: Roll small pieces of dough into 3-inch circles, place a spoonful of filling in the center, and pleat the edges to seal, leaving a small opening at the top.

Step 4:Steam the Dumplings: Arrange the dumplings in a steamer lined with parchment paper and steam over boiling water for 8-10 minutes.

Step 5:Cook noodles as directed.

Step 6:Serve: Serve the dumplings hot with a dipping sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, and sliced ginger.

Source: Xiao Long Bao (Soup Dumplings)

Meal Ingredients
Shansi Leng Mian (Eel Noodles)
    Noodles:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • Eel Sauce:
  • 1 pound fresh eel, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup scallions, chopped
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth

Step 1: Prepare the Noodles: Mix flour, water, and salt to form a dough, knead until smooth, cover, and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Step 2:Make the Eel Sauce: In a pan, sauté ginger and garlic until fragrant, add eel pieces, soy sauce, rice wine, sugar, and broth, then simmer until the eel is cooked through.

Step 3:Roll and Cut the Noodles: Roll out the rested dough into thin sheets and cut into noodle strips.

Step 4: Cook the Noodles: Boil the noodle strips in salted water until cooked, then drain.

Step 5:Serve: Toss the cooked noodles with the eel sauce, garnish with chopped scallions, and serve hot.

Source: Eel Noodle
